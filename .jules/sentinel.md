## 2026-04-18 - Blocklist Evasion via Spoofed Caller in SELFDESTRUCT
**Vulnerability:** The Arc Network's customized `SELFDESTRUCT` opcode logic (`arc_network_selfdestruct_impl`) validates the executing contract's address (`source`) and the destination address (`target`) against the compliance blocklist, but completely ignores the `caller_address` (`msg.sender`).
**Learning:** In standard EVM execution, it is often sufficient to check the source and target because the execution originates directly from the caller. However, within the unique subcall architecture of Arc Network, precompiles like `CallFrom` can dispatch execution frames with arbitrary, spoofed `caller_address` fields. This disjointed context allows an attacker to orchestrate a `SELFDESTRUCT` from a non-blocklisted proxy contract while the underlying sanctioned user acts as the spoofed caller, bypassing the compliance checks.
**Prevention:** Always evaluate all relevant context addresses (`source`, `target`, AND `caller`) when validating compliance constraints in a heavily customized EVM engine. The `CallFrom` precompile breaks traditional EVM assumptions about the relationship between execution frames and their originators.
